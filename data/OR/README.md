# Oregon data
Oregon's property tax system is governed by Measure 5 and Measure 50, two constitutional amendments passed in 1990 and 1997, respectivly. See League of Oregon Cities, "[FAQ on Measures 5 & 50](https://www.orcities.org/application/files/2216/8685/9599/FAQonMeasures5and_50-updated5-23.pdf)," Mar. 2023. Residential and commercial properties are valued at 100 percent of their assessed value, which is the lower of th real market value (RMV) or the maximum assessed value (MAV). Measure 50 requires that the MAV cannot grow by more than 3% per year, unless there is new construction or other qualifying improvements.

"Property taxes are set by and paid to the county assessor," not the State of Oregon Department of Revenue. See State of Oregon Department of Revenue "[Property Tax](https://www.oregon.gov/dor/programs/property/pages/default.aspx)" page, visited Jun. 22, 2026.

"County assessors appraise most Oregon property. The Department of Revenue appraises certain large industrial sites and utility properties. The value of property is determined as of January 1 of each year.... All personal property must be valued at 100 percent of its real market value unless otherwise exempt (ORS 307.020). Personal property is taxable in the county where it's located as of January 1 at 1 a.m." See Oregon Department of Revenue "[Property assessment and taxation](https://www.oregon.gov/dor/programs/property/Pages/Personal-Property.aspx)" page, accessed Jun. 22, 2026.

As with many data centers across the country, Oregon's existing data centers (e.g., Google, Amazon, and Meta) have taken advantage temporary property tax abatements from Oregon's Enterprise Zone programs. Enterprise Zones can exempt new buildings and equipment from local property taxes for several years, after which the property becomes fully taxable. See Business Oregon's "[Enterprise Zones](https://www.oregon.gov/biz/programs/enterprisezones/pages/default.aspx)" web page, accessed Jun. 22, 2026.

The compression (downward revision) is significant from Measure 5 valuation reductions in order to meet the limits of $5 per $1,000 of real market value for education districts and $10 per $1,000 of real market value for general government districts. The data are found in the Oregon Department of Revenue, "[Oregon Property Tax Statistics](https://www.oregon.gov/dor/gov-research/pages/property_tax_statistics.aspx)" page, accessed Jun. 23, 2026, in the "[FY 2024-25 Oregon property tax statistics report](https://www.oregon.gov/dor/programs/gov-research/Documents/FY%202024-25%20Oregon%20Property%20Tax%20Statistics%20Report%20150-303-405-Secured.pdf)." We use the "[2024-25 Property Tax Statistics Detail Tables (Excel)](https://www.oregon.gov/dor/programs/gov-research/Documents/FY%202024-25%20Property%20Tax%20Statistics%20Detail%20Tables.xlsx)," which we have also saved as the [`/data/OR/FY 2024-25 Property Tax Statistics Detail Tables.xlsx`](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/data/OR/FY%202024-25%20Property%20Tax%20Statistics%20Detail%20Tables.xlsx) file, Table 1.6, in the in open source GitHub repository for the DataCenterAtlas.org web tool.

Because the Measure 5 compression (downward revisions) are significant to property assessment valuations--which can be easily seen by comparing the "Measure 5 Value (M5V)" to the "Net Assessed Value (NAV)" variables in the [`/data/OR/FY 2024-25 Property Tax Statistics Detail Tables.xlsx`](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/data/OR/FY%202024-25%20Property%20Tax%20Statistics%20Detail%20Tables.xlsx) file--we use the "Average Tax Rate ($ per 1000) M5V Base FY 2024-25" variable as our average effective tax rates by county. The M5V valuation is closest to real market value (see note at bottom of Table 1.6).

Our code for extracting and calculating the average effective property tax rates by county in Oregon for the 2024-2025 year is in the [`/code/OR/or_analyses.py`](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/code/OR/or_analyses.py) file in the open source GitHub repository for the DataCenterAtlas.org web tool. The data for Oregon average effective property tax rates by county for the year 2024-2025 is saved as [`/data/OR/cnty_prop_tax_rates_or_2025.csv`](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/data/OR/cnty_prop_tax_rates_or_2025.csv).
