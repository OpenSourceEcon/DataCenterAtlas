# California data
To calculate California average effective property tax rates by county. we use the following dataset for 2025 from California State Board of Equalization website's "[Property Tax Allocations (Table 14, 15)](https://www.boe.ca.gov/dataportal/dataset.htm?url=PropTaxGenPropTaxLevies)" page (https://www.boe.ca.gov/dataportal/dataset.htm?url=PropTaxGenPropTaxLevies).

- 2025 "[Property Tax Allocations (Table 14, 15)](https://www.boe.ca.gov/dataportal/dataset.htm?url=PropTaxGenPropTaxLevies)", Californai State Board of Equilization (accessed, June 16, 2026). We only use the first 58 rows of this file because the rows after that are for previous years. [[CSV file](https://www.boe.ca.gov/dataportal/dataset.htm?url=PropTaxGenPropTaxLevies) on California State Board of Equalization site] [[CSV file](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/data/CA/PropTaxGenPropTaxLevies.csv) on DataCenterAtlas.org GitHub repository]

In California, the property tax system for both commercial property and residential property is dominated by Proposition 13, in place since 1978, that cut and capped property taxes across the state. (See NAIOP, "[Prop 13](https://www.naiop.org/advocacy/state-local-issues/prop-13)" NAIOP Commercial Real Estate Development Association, accessed June 16, 2026.)
- Property is initially assessed at approximately its market value when acquired or newly constructed.
- The assessed value can increase by at most 2% per year (or inflation, if lower) until a change in ownership or new construction triggers reassessment.
- The statewide base property tax rate is 1% of assessed value, plus voter-approved local assessments and bond levies.

Similar to other states, California taxes both land and building, as well as business tangible personal property (TPP) like servers network equipment, electrical equipment, and fixtures. These TPP assets are all part of the fair market value of a commercial property, and are revalued each year according to approved depreciation schedules (See California State Board of Equalization, "[Personal Property - Frequently Asked Questions](https://www.boe.ca.gov/proptaxes/faqs/faqsprop-pp.htm)" page.)

To calcuate the average effective property tax in each California County, we use the "Average Tax Rate" variable from the [`PropTaxGenPropTaxLevies.csv`](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/data/CA/PropTaxGenPropTaxLevies.csv) spreadsheet, which represents the total property tax levies from county, city, school, and other special districts divided by the net taxable assessed value of all the properties in that county over the 2024-to-2025 period.

It is likely given the Proposition 13 rules that residential properties are assessed less frequently than commercial properties. If the value of both are increasing, this would mean that residential properties are taxed at a lower effective tax rate. This would therefore mean that our calculated average effective property tax rate is a lower bound for the actual property tax rate faced by data centers. But our use of the "net taxable value" variable as the denominatory in our tax rate calculation mitigates this bias.
