# Texas data
To calculate Texas average effective property tax rates by county. we gather the following four datasets for 2025 from the Texas Comptroller's website property taxes "[Tax Rates and Levies]((https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/rates/index.php))" page (https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/rates/index.php).

- 2025 School District Rates and Levies ([`2025-school-district-rates-levies.xlsx`](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/docs/2025-school-district-rates-levies.xlsx)) ([Texas Comptroller site](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/docs/2025-school-district-rates-levies.xlsx); DataCenterAtlas.org [GitHub repository](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/data/TX/2025-school-district-rates-levies.xlsx))
- 2025 City Rates and Levies ([`2025-city-rates-levies.xlsx`](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/docs/2025-city-rates-levies.xlsx)) ([Texas Comptroller site](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/docs/2025-city-rates-levies.xlsx); DataCenterAtlas.org [GitHub repository](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/data/TX/2025-city-rates-levies.xlsx))
- 2025 County Rates and Levies ([`2025-city-rates-levies.xlsx`](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/docs/2025-county-rates-levies.xlsx)) ([Texas Comptroller site](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/docs/2025-county-rates-levies.xlsx); DataCenterAtlas.org [GitHub repository](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/data/TX/2025-county-rates-levies.xlsx))
- 2025 Special District Rates and Levies ([`2025-special-district-rates-levies.xlsx`](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/docs/2025-special-district-rates-levies.xlsx)) ([Texas Comptroller site](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/docs/2025-special-district-rates-levies.xlsx); DataCenterAtlas.org [GitHub repository](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/data/TX/2025-special-district-rates-levies.xlsx))

To calcuate the average effective property tax in each Texas County, we add up all the calculated levy amounts for each county across the four datasets, then divide by the total taxable property value (assessed values) across each county. For the total assessed property value, we use the maximum value across each dataset. We don't use the sum across all four datasets for the total taxable property value because each spreadsheet has an estimate of that value individually. Our code for generating these average effective property tax rates is [`code/TX/tx_county_rates.py`](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/code/TX/tx_county_rates.py). We have saved the resulting average effective property tax rates by county in the file [`data/TX/cnty_prop_tax_rates_tx_2025.csv`](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/data/TX/cnty_prop_tax_rates_tx_2025.csv).

This approach likely underestimates the average effective tax rates faced by commercial properties. Texas appraises both residential and commercial property at 100% of market value, but homeowners receive several benefits that commercial property generally does not. (See Williamson County Central Appraisal District web page, "[Market Value and the Homestead Cap](https://www.wcad.org/hs-cap)," https://www.wcad.org/hs-cap, accessed June 11, 2026.) Our approach of calculating the average effective rate as the sum of calculated levies divided by the total taxable property value deducts the tax benefits afforded residential owners, and therefore gets closer to the tax rates faced by data centers.

- Homestead cap: limit of $140,000 taxable value for school taxes; 10% annual growth appraisal cap. (See Collin County Central Appraisal District web page, "[What is a Homestead Cap](https://collincad.org/faqs/what-is-a-homestead-cap)," https://collincad.org/faqs/what-is-a-homestead-cap, accessed June 11, 2026.)
- Circuit breaker cap: non-homestead commercial properties valued under $5.3 million limit to 20% annual growth appraisal cap. (See Joyce Beebe, John W. Diamond, and Jennifer Raab, "[The Surprising Impact of the 20% Appraisal Cap in Texas](https://www.bakerinstitute.org/research/surprising-impact-20-appraisal-cap-texas)," Research Paper, Center for Tax and Budget Policy, Baker Institute for Public Policy, Rice University, Sep. 9, 2025.)

## Alternative method
An alternative method for calculating Texas average effective property tax rates by county is to add up the overlapping jurisdictions of property tax rates in different areas of each county, then average those rates. TheT exas Comptroller's website [property taxes page](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/rates/index.php) has a file for 2025 called "Cities, Counties, School Districts, and Special Districts" ([`2025-total-rates-levies.xlsx`](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/docs/2025-total-rates-levies.xlsx)) ([Texas Comptroller site](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/property-tax/docs/2025-total-rates-levies.xlsx), DataCenterAtlas.org [GitHub repository](https://github.com/OpenSourceEcon/DataCenterAtlas/blob/main/data/TX/2025-total-rates-levies.xlsx)).

This file has the property tax rates for all the overlapping jursidictions in each Texas county. The difficulty is discovering what geographic boundaries each taxing jurisdiction applies to. It is hard to know which tax rates add up, and which rates are separate. Being able to follow this method would be a more accurate average effective property tax rate for commercial data centers. But we do not have enough information at present to pursue this method.
